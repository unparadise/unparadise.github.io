Simplify Teams Admin Center

  • Role: Lead product designer
  • Responsibilities: Customer feedback analysis, experience audit, design concept explorations, prototyping, and design spec.
  • Year: 2023-2024

Outcomes:

  1. Increased overall policy management user SAT by 15%.
  2. Reduced delete a policy with assigned users task from 13 to 4 steps.
  3. Reduced the time to find the right policy and settings in policy tab on user details page by 30%.

Background

Teams Admin Center is the website IT admins use to manage how users in his or her organization can use Microsoft Teams. It has approx. 270 MAU with 44.2% from small to medium business, 28.7% from enterprise, , 15.6% from small to medium corporation, and 11.5% from education.

The team has been tracking NPS over the past year. TAC's NPS (measured every month) has been hovering around 35 on average for all customer segments for the past year. In comparison, SharePoint Admin Center (SAC) has a NPS around 55 and Microsoft 365 Admin Center (MAC) has a NPS around 50.4. It's obvious that TAC's score is behind both other Microsoft Admin Centers and the industry average. As a result, the business set the goal to improve customer satisfaction in fiscal year 2024.

As the senior product manager on the team, my responsibility includes addressing the immediate look & feel update of the inbox apps to generate energy and create product differentiation by discovering unmet user needs. Through user research and data analysis, I identified key pain points and opportunities for improvement, such as the need for better screen capturing and multi-file editing tools.

Based on this research, I developed a roadmap for the portfolio of apps that aligned with the Windows strategy.

Focus Sessions Feature

One of these features was the Windows Focus Sessions, which I pitched, incubated and led the development of. This feature helps users to be more productive and stay healthy by integrating with the Windows operating system to provide customizable work and break intervals, task tracking, and ambient music.

To create a multi-disciplinary team, I brought together researchers from Microsoft Research, product managers from Windows Shell and Lab teams, designers, and user researchers. Employing a hypothesis-driven framework, we identified user pain-points and collaborated closely with research to validate design concepts.




I spearheaded the development of the Windows Focus Experience strategy and roadmap, involving key stakeholders from Windows Shell, To Do app, Your Phone, and MyAnalytics (now Viva Insights). This roadmap continued to guide the feature development even after my departure from the team.


Throughout the process, I actively sought feedback from internal stakeholders and end users, using it to iterate on feature requirements and refine product design.




The result was a highly successful feature that has received positive reviews from users and industry experts alike. The Windows Focus Sessions feature has helped users to be more productive and stay healthy, and it has been announced by Panos as a game changer in Windows 11. It was also adopted by the Viva Insight product later.
